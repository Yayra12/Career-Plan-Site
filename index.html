Biomedical Engineers

What They Do

Apply knowledge of engineering, biology, chemistry, computer science, and biomechanical principles to the design, development, and evaluation of biological, agricultural, and health systems and products, such as artificial organs, prostheses, instrumentation, medical information systems, and health management and care delivery systems.

  • Conduct research, along with life scientists, chemists, and medical scientists, on the engineering aspects of the biological systems of humans and animals.
  • Adapt or design computer hardware or software for medical science uses.
  • Evaluate the safety, efficiency, and effectiveness of biomedical equipment.

Skills and Knowledge Needed

  • Problem solving skils
  • figuring out how a system should work and how changes in the future will affect it
  • Knowledge in computers and electronics
  • Knowledge in arithmetic, algebra, geometry, calculus, or statistics, biology

Abilities/Personality/Technology

  • Abilities: Verbal, ideas and logic, math, visual unerstanding
  • Personality: Integrity, cooperation, analytical thinking, persistence
    • Technology Used: Analytical or scientific software, computer aided design CAD software, enterprise application integration software

Education and Pay

  • Bachelor or Master's degree
  • Average pay of $100,730

Why you should choose this career

Biomedical engineering is a hotbed of innovation. You'll have the opportunity to brainstorm and create groundbreaking solutions to complex medical problems, fostering your creativity and problem-solving skills.Healthcare challenges are universal, and biomedical engineering allows you to address them on a global scale. Whether it's designing affordable prosthetics for developing countries or developing advanced imaging techniques, your work can have far-reaching effects.
